## So what — strategic implications

- **"Shift to production" is no longer a forecast — it's the show floor.** Figure at 1 robot/hour, Digit on commercial RaaS at Toyota, Boston Dynamics Atlas in volume delivery: the transition narrative is closing. Pilot budgets at named OEM customers are converting to capex.
- **NVIDIA is building an inescapable gravity well.** ABB's 99%-accurate sim-to-real pipeline, GR00T N1.7 in commercial access, and sponsored Humanoid Pavilion all run on NVIDIA silicon and software. Each partnership makes defection costly for OEMs. Intel needs a concrete Automate counter-story — a named OEM design win on Core Ultra / OpenVINO — before this show closes June 25.
- **Kawasaki's open KRNX API is the right architecture for hybrid-edge.** External-orchestration-first, ML-agnostic, ROS 2-compatible: the RL030N can run any edge-AI stack without hardware replacement. This is the wedge where Intel OpenVINO could still compete on installed industrial cobots — if Intel moves fast.
- **China's unit-volume lead compounds while the West showcases.** Unitree + AgiBot near 80% global humanoid share. No Automate announcement today directly addresses BOM cost parity. Watch for any Chinese OEM presence or partnership disclosures through June 25.
