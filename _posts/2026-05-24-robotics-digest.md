## 7. Policy / standards / safety

- **DARPA PhysComp RFI — deadline May 27** (3 days). "Materials for Physical Compute in Untethered Robotics" (DARPA-SN-26-76, MTO). Seeks materials that integrate sensing, actuation, *and* computation into a single physical substrate — no central processor. The vision: robots that respond in real time without data movement to an NPU or GPU. Selected respondents invited to an in-person workshop, summer 2026. [[DARPA](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2026/rethinking-robotics)] [[HPCwire](https://www.hpcwire.com/off-the-wire/darpa-issues-rfi-on-embedding-intelligence-into-robotic-materials/)]

- **EU AI Act — full applicability August 2, 2026** (10 weeks). Autonomous robots broadly classified as "high-risk." Firms must also comply simultaneously with the Machinery Regulation (MDR) — dual certification burden. [[Bird & Bird analysis](https://www.twobirds.com/en/insights/2026/smart-robots,-dual-regulations-navigating-the-ai-act-and-machinery-compliance)] [[EU AI Act portal](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)]

- **ISO 10218 rev** in joint CEN/ISO revision; draft circulating. No new action in last 24 h.

## So what — strategic implications

- **Quiet weekend before an announcement-dense week.** Robotics Summit pre-event embargoes lift Monday; ICRA workshops start publishing proceedings. Intel, NVIDIA, Qualcomm, and robotics OEMs routinely time press releases to coincide with these events. Watch for silicon/software partnership announcements early week.

- **DARPA's "intelligence in materials" framing is a slow-burn challenge to Intel's hybrid-edge story.** If future robots can perceive and act through neuromorphic/smart-material substrates, the case for centralized NPU compute (Core Ultra, Jetson Thor) becomes domain-specific rather than universal. This is a 5–10 year horizon threat, not 2026 — but DARPA programs have a habit of reshaping roadmaps earlier than expected. Hybrid-edge remains the right bet for this product cycle.

- **Dual compliance cliff (China July 1 / EU August 2) is a go-to-market gate.** Any OEM shipping humanoids to both markets faces simultaneous documentation sprints. Firms with heterogeneous compute stacks (on-robot edge + nearby server + cloud) need separate validation trails for each regime. This is a deployment-friction moat for established players with compliance infrastructure.

- **Unitree STAR IPO is the key China signal to watch.** A successful ¥4.2 B listing would reset Western valuation benchmarks for humanoid OEMs, likely accelerating AgiBot and Fourier filings. It would also redirect Chinese institutional capital more formally into the sector — watch for follow-on domestic fund announcements post-listing.
