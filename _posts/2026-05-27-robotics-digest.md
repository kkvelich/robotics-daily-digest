## 7. Policy / standards / safety

- **DARPA RFI deadline — today:** DARPA-SN-26-76 "Materials for Physical Compute in Untethered Robotics" (Microsystems Technology Office) closed responses at **2 p.m. ET, May 27**. The RFI asks the research community to define materials that can **intermix sensing, adaptation, and actuation in the physical substrate** — robots that sense and react through their structure without relying on centralized processors or cloud links. Selected respondents will be invited to a **summer 2026 workshop**; a formal BAA for research contracts follows. This is a 5–10 year program arc, not near-term product. [[DARPA news]](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2026/rethinking-robotics) [[HPCwire]](https://www.hpcwire.com/off-the-wire/darpa-issues-rfi-on-embedding-intelligence-into-robotic-materials/) [[TechTimes]](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/316863/20260519/darpa-wants-robots-that-sense-react-through-their-materials-not-processors-submissions-due-may.htm)

## So what — strategic implications

- **Unitree's Q1 profit squeeze is the sector's first public stress test.** Revenue growing at 68 % while adjusted profit drops 52 % exposes the cost of racing to scale embodied-AI R&D and sales simultaneously. If SSE's June 1 hearing passes the IPO, it validates a "long-duration growth" narrative for Chinese humanoids; a deferral would pressure valuations across the pre-IPO cohort (AgiBot, Fourier, LimX). Watch the June 1 verdict as a sentiment indicator for the whole China humanoid wave.

- **Open-source ROS/OSRA gaining institutional floor.** Gerkey's Robotics Summit keynote — timed as the sector's premier developer event opens — is a deliberate framing move: open infrastructure vs. proprietary physical-AI stacks. Intel's OpenVINO integration with ROS 2 is the right strategic bet here; the OSRA momentum strengthens the case for meeting developers on familiar, open toolchains rather than competing on closed frameworks.

- **DARPA's materials-compute RFI flags a long-horizon disruption.** The explicit framing — robots that don't rely on "continuous external computation" or centralized processors — is a direct challenge to the NPU-centric edge architecture thesis. It won't affect the 2026–2028 product cycle, but executives investing in silicon lock-in should monitor how DARPA's workshop shapes the next BAA.

- **Watch Summit Day 2 (May 28):** Boston Dynamics, Agility Robotics, General Motors, and Universal Robots all keynoting. Expect deployment-scale and software-platform news that will feed tomorrow's sweep.
