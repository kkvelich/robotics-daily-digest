## So what — strategic implications

- **China's production gap is now a moat, not just a lead.** AGIBOT at 15,000 units (accelerating) means Chinese semi-humanoids are competing on BOM cost and throughput. Western players (Agility ~Digit v5 pre-order phase, Figure 03 at ~1 unit/hour) are operating in a fundamentally different cost structure. Expect Chinese pricing pressure on 2H 2026 enterprise contracts to become the central competitive variable.

- **June 30 is a data event.** If MIIT publishes aggregate deployment-plan submissions or sector breakdowns, it will be the first quantitative read on which Chinese industries are actually committing humanoid robots to production — manufacturing vs. logistics vs. healthcare mix shapes compute architecture demand (real-time manipulation → high NPU, logistics → lower-power AMR-class SoCs).

- **Intel's edge-robotics window is closing on Chinese OEM design wins.** AGIBOT G2 compute is undisclosed, but China's supply-chain gravity pulls toward Horizon Robotics, Cambricon, and Rockchip for on-robot inference. Intel's OpenVINO Physical AI (Computex, June 1) and its 130+ Series 3 design engagements are largely Western-skewed. Without announced Chinese design wins before suppliers lock H2 2026 BOMs, Intel cedes the world's highest-volume humanoid production environment to domestic Chinese silicon.

- **Watch this week:** (1) MIIT aggregate data from June 30 submission deadline; (2) Agility Robotics SPAC SEC Form S-4 filing (post-June 24 announcement); (3) Unitree IPO prospectus updates on the STAR Market; (4) AGIBOT disclosure of G2 compute stack as the robot enters broader customer deployments.
