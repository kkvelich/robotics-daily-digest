## So what — strategic implications

- **Monday's Automate floor is the highest-signal event of the week.** Every major cobot and AMR OEM shows up; the compute silicon each names in their hardware spec sheet is a direct read on the competitive trajectory for H2 2026 deployments.
- **Intel's Automate showing is make-or-break for the OpenVINO Physical AI narrative.** 130+ "design wins" were claimed at Computex — Automate is where those need to become identifiable customer names on live hardware. If they don't appear publicly here, the competitive story defaults to NVIDIA (Jetson Thor) and Qualcomm (Dragonwing with its newly cleaned-up BSP).
- **NEURA Robotics' Automate debut is the funding-to-deployment bridge test.** A $1.4B Series C company with Amazon and NVIDIA as investors showing a "Neuraverse" platform (multi-form-factor cobots + transports + humanoids in one orchestration layer) is the most ambitious product claim at the show. If the live demos are credible, it resets customer expectations for what "full-stack robotics" must mean.
- **Hellbender's Physical AI camera line is the sleeper.** On-edge camera systems with built-in AI acceleration and 64-camera array support at seed-round price points could undercut the typical multi-vendor sensor+compute integration tax on factory-floor robotics — worth tracking as a BOM compressor.
