## So what — strategic implications

- **AGIBOT's livestream close is the first publicly audited deployment-grade humanoid benchmark.** 310 UPH, >99% OEE, 6 days continuous, third-party factory floor — no Western competitor has an equivalent public data point. Figure AI, 1X, and Apptronik have cited private pilot metrics; AGIBOT now has a number procurement teams can put in an RFP. Expect others to replicate this format in Q3.

- **The production ramp math is striking:** 5k→10k→15k in two consecutive 3-month windows means AGIBOT is sustaining ~1,600 units/month, roughly 4× its 2025 monthly average. If this rate holds, they exit 2026 at 20k+ cumulative — and China's mandated 10k deployment target starts to look undersized relative to available supply.

- **Today's filing deadline forces demand-side action.** The real question is no longer whether China can produce humanoid robots — AGIBOT and Unitree have answered that. The question is whether the deployment plans filed today translate to real operational use cases by November. Watch for Q3 customer deployment announcements from state-owned enterprise partners as the implementation clock starts.

- **Intel's hybrid-edge gap in China remains open.** AGIBOT's G2 silicon is undisclosed; Unitree's stack has leaned toward domestic silicon (Horizon, Cambricon). With 15,000 robots in the field from one vendor alone, the compute procurement decision for the next wave — and it will be a large wave — is being made now without Intel named in any public win. A China partnership announcement or reference design win in a named humanoid OEM would be a significant signal.
