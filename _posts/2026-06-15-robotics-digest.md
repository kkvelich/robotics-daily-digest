## So what — strategic implications

1. **$30K consumer humanoid demand is real, at least in China.** 4,000 pre-orders and $1.4M in deposits before the product is even officially launched validates a companion/elder-care price tier that most industry models treated as hypothetical until 2028. The unit economics ($30K MSRP × 88 DoF) are very different from industrial humanoids ($100K+), and the compute requirement (emotional AI, local encrypted memory, Wi-Fi) is closer to a consumer appliance than a factory robot. Qualcomm / MediaTek are better positioned for this tier than NVIDIA or Intel.

2. **Burro Grande's "1M+ real-world hours" claim is the AMR market's most underrated moat.** In a segment full of well-funded demos, field-validated reliability data is scarce and expensive to build. Burro's progression from ag-carry to industrial towing mirrors the classic AMR land-and-expand playbook. The move to industrial facilities opens a much larger TAM than agriculture alone.

3. **Pre-Automate silence from Intel is notable.** The biggest NA robotics show opens in seven days with a full NVIDIA-sponsored humanoid pavilion. Intel has not made a robotics silicon announcement in several weeks. A named humanoid or logistics-robot design win announced on the Automate floor would be impactful; arriving without one cedes the narrative to NVIDIA for another quarter.

4. **China's consumer + industrial + IPO track is now fully integrated.** UBTECH drives consumer demand (U1), EngineAI drives industrial scale (1 T800/15 min), Unitree drives public-market validation (first embodied AI IPO). This is a vertically integrated ecosystem cycle moving faster than any comparable Western effort. The competitive clock is measured in months, not years.
