## So what — strategic implications

1. **Jetson Thor is the confirmed H1 2026 robotics compute standard**: Golden Award + GR00T Reference Humanoid architecture + 1X NEO factory spec + Advantech/DFI/ASUS OEM ecosystem = highest platform density in edge robotics. Intel's 130 design wins are the most credible competitive signal, but ecosystem gap is 12+ months wide. The conversion rate of those 130 to shipping products in H2 is the metric that matters for Intel's edge-robotics revenue story.

2. **BMW Leipzig is the European deployment proof point this summer**: AEON in EV battery assembly is the first Physical AI test in European series production. Hyundai/DeepMind (Atlas) and Toyota/Agility (Digit) are the analogues in North America. A successful summer pilot at BMW opens the EU automotive gateway — a much larger TAM than current US pilots.

3. **BYD's "no plans to sell" is the most strategically informative China signal today**: The original dealer-network story created a narrative of imminent humanoid commoditization via EV distribution. 36Kr's clarification resets that. But BYD as its own 20K-unit customer + Xi'an plant still represents the largest vertically-integrated production bet in the sector — the competitive threat is in BOM and supply-chain structure, not dealer distribution timing.

4. **Watch this week**: AgiBot June 13 consumer store (demand signal); Physical Intelligence $1B round (Founders Fund + Lightspeed still in late-stage talks per March reporting); ICRA 2026 best-paper formal announcements; BMW Leipzig AEON first operational update.
