## 7. Policy / standards / safety

- **US One Big Beautiful Bill Act signed (July 4)**: Manufacturing equipment — including industrial robots, cobots, conveyors, welding systems, and palletizers — now qualifies for 100% bonus depreciation in the year placed in service, through 2029. Section 179 deduction cap raised $1.25M → $2.5M (phase-out begins at $4M). New Qualified Production Property (QPP) deduction for US-based manufacturing facilities may allow full write-off of facility construction. Net effect: a six-figure robot purchase creates an immediate six-figure tax benefit. [(StandardBots analysis)](https://standardbots.com/blog/obbb-robotics) (date via search index) · [(Tax Foundation FAQ)](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/one-big-beautiful-bill-act-tax-changes/)
- **EU AI Act — 29-day clock**: High-risk AI system obligations take effect August 2, 2026. Robots used as safety components in manufacturing, logistics, or healthcare automatically fall under the high-risk regime. Harmonized CEN-CENELEC standards not expected until Q4 2026, creating a compliance gap. [(HI AI Design)](https://www.hiai-design.com/blog-eu-ai-act-robotics-2026) · [(Interoperable Europe)](https://interoperable-europe.ec.europa.eu/collection/rolling-plan-ict-standardisation/robotics-and-autonomous-systems-rp-2026)

## So what — strategic implications

- **Unitree's STAR Market listing sets a public-market benchmark for humanoid valuations**: 60% gross margin and positive cash generation at 5,500 units/year is the baseline investors will use to price every next-round for Figure, Agility, 1X, and the rest of the field. If the IPO prices at a premium, it will pull forward fundraising timelines across the sector; if it disappoints, expect a cooling in late-2026 term sheets.
- **OBBBA 100% depreciation changes the enterprise buy-vs-wait calculus through 2029**: US customers evaluating cobot or AMR fleet purchases can now fully expense capital in year one. Expect accelerated Q3/Q4 purchase cycles; integrators and OEMs targeting US enterprise should model this into their pipeline.
- **EU AI Act August 2 is the near-term compliance crunch**: Robotics OEMs with EU high-risk applications (surgical, collaborative industrial, logistics safety-critical) have 29 days and no harmonized standards to anchor to. Compliance posture will vary widely; product liability exposure is real for unprepared vendors.
- **WAIC July 17–20 is the next high-signal event for the China edge-compute watch**: Horizon Robotics, Black Sesame, and Cambricon announcements at WAIC could materially shift the Chinese silicon stack narrative — particularly relevant for understanding whether US export controls are accelerating or slowing domestic alternatives.
